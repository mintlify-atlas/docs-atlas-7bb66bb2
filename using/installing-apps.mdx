---
title: "Installing apps"
description: "How to install, open, uninstall, and manage apps in Accrescent."
---

Accrescent installs apps directly from the store without requiring an account. Every package is cryptographically verified before installation.

## Installing an app

<Steps>
  <Step title="Find the app">
    Open the **All Apps** tab and scroll to the app you want, or tap a direct link to open its details screen.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Tap Install">
    On the app details screen, tap the **Install** button. Accrescent immediately enqueues a download worker and begins fetching the package.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Wait for the download">
    The **Cancel** button replaces **Install** while the download is in progress. A circular progress indicator overlays the app icon, and a status line shows the current percentage and total file size.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Installation completes">
    Once the package is verified and committed to the Android installer, the progress indicator clears and the action buttons update to **Uninstall** and **Open**.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  No account or sign-in is required. Accrescent never asks for personal information to install an app.
</Note>

## Tracking progress

During a download you can see two forms of feedback:

- **Progress ring** — A circular indicator overlays the app icon. It shows exact download progress as a filled arc once byte-level progress data becomes available. Before that, it shows as an indeterminate spinner.
- **Status text** — A line below the version string shows the percentage complete and the total download size, for example: `42.0% of 18 MB`.

While the package is being installed (after the download finishes), both the status text and indicator switch to an indeterminate state showing "Installing…".

## Cancelling a download

Tap **Cancel** at any point during the download to abort. The **Cancel** button is enabled while the download is enqueued or running. It becomes temporarily disabled once the package has been handed off to the Android installer — at that point the installation cannot be interrupted.

After cancellation, the details screen shows a brief notice that the download was cancelled, and the **Install** button reappears.

## Opening an installed app

Once an app is installed and enabled, an **Open** button appears on its details screen. Tap **Open** to launch the app directly from Accrescent.

<Tip>
  The **Open** button only appears for apps that have a launchable activity. System-level or background-only apps may show only **Uninstall**.
</Tip>

## Enabling a disabled app

If an app is installed but disabled on your device, the details screen shows an **Enable** button instead of **Open**. Tapping **Enable** opens the Android system **App info** screen for that app, where you can re-enable it.

<Note>
  On Android 14 (API 34) and above, Accrescent can update a disabled app without requiring you to re-enable it first. On earlier versions, you must enable the app before an update can be applied.
</Note>

## Uninstalling an app

Tap **Uninstall** on the app details screen to remove the app from your device. Android's standard uninstall flow handles the removal. After uninstallation, the details screen returns to showing the **Install** button.

<Warning>
  Uninstalling an app removes all of its data from your device. This action cannot be undone from within Accrescent.
</Warning>

## App archiving (Android 15+)

On Android 15 (API 35) and above, the Android system can archive apps to free up storage while preserving the app's data and icon. Archived apps are not fully uninstalled; they are suspended and can be restored.

When an app is archived, the **Install** button is replaced with **Unarchive**. Tapping **Unarchive** restores the app through the Android system archiving mechanism.

## Installation errors

If an installation fails, an error box appears on the details screen. Common errors include:

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="App incompatible">
    The app's package requirements are not met by your device. This may be due to an unsupported CPU architecture or Android version.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="App store unreachable">
    Accrescent could not connect to the store server. Check your network connection and try again.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Package verification failed">
    The downloaded package did not pass cryptographic verification. This protects you from tampered or corrupted files. Try installing again; if the problem persists, report it.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Storage error">
    Your device does not have enough free storage to complete the installation. Free up space and try again.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Blocked by another package">
    Another app on your device is preventing the installation. The error message identifies the blocking package when possible.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Timeout">
    The download or installation took too long to complete. Try again on a faster or more stable network connection.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

Tap the dismiss icon on the error box to clear it. The action buttons return to their previous state so you can retry.
